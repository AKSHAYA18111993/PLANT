Frequently Asked Questions

The ideal placement for your indoor plants depends on their light requirements. Generally, most indoor plants thrive in bright, indirect light. Avoid direct sunlight for plants that prefer lower light, and ensure good air circulation.
To encourage faster growth, provide adequate light, consistent watering (without overwatering), appropriate humidity, and regular fertilization during the growing season. Ensure they are in well-draining soil and a pot with drainage holes.
Regularly inspect your plants for pests. If you find any, isolate the plant and treat it with neem oil, insecticidal soap, or other organic pest control methods. Good air circulation and proper watering can also help prevent infestations.
Yes, many indoor plants are known to purify the air by absorbing common indoor pollutants like formaldehyde, benzene, and trichloroethylene, and releasing oxygen. While their effect on a large scale might be limited, they contribute to a healthier indoor environment.
Watering frequency depends on the plant type, pot size, humidity, and season. A good rule of thumb is to check the top inch or two of soil; if it feels dry, it's time to water. Avoid overwatering, which can lead to root rot.

Our Story

Hey there! I'm Siddhant Bhalinge, the Founder and CEO of Ray and I'd love to share our story with you.

It's a story of passion, perseverance, and a deep love for nature that's been passed down in my family for three generations.

Our story began with Namdeo Umaji. The oldest seed company in India, founded by my great-grandfather in 1885.

As a young boy, I spent my days in his Byculla shop, surrounded by the promise of life contained in tiny seeds. Those formative years really were the stepping stones of Ray's own journey.
